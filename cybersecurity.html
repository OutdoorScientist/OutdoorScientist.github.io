Cybersecurity

I investigated malware detection on IoT devices by analyzing power consumption and using logged data to train a machine learning model that flagged infected devices. This involved researching and infecting devices with malware such as SSH BotNets, Ransomware, and Keylogger, and developing the first evasion techniques for power-based side-channel detection.

Additionally, I researched and presented methods to bypass mobile app security permissions, demonstrating how these security restrictions can be circumvented to a group of professors and PhD candidates.

IoT Malware Detection

Investigated if Internet of Thing (IoT) devices could be flagged as having malware based off of power consumption (i.e. side channel malware monitoring). To achieve this, malware was installed on multiple IoT devices while the both the central node's and individual IoT devices power consumption were being logged. These power consumption logs were then used to train a machine learning model in order to flag affected IoT devices.

  • Analyzed the state of IoT devices malware and its current financial losses due to malware.
  • Researched and then infected varying devices with SSH BotNets, RansomWare, and KeyLoggers.
  • Went through a series of revisions as it was discovered that if a power supply is not large enough even small tasks would max out the power drawn.
  • To the best of our knowledge, we were the first researchers to develop evasion techniques for power based side channel consumption on IoT devices.
