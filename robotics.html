Robotic Projects

I designed and began constructing a heavy-duty robotic arm capable of lifting 10 pounds over a 1.5-foot reach. The project involved researching robotic arm mechanics, motors, servos, H-bridges, and Raspberry Pi integration, with a focus on linear actuators and system constraints. Additionally, I built and upgraded electric longboards, retrofitting an electric motor and custom battery packs. I achieved a 7 mph speed and built battery packs through extensive research on lithium batteries, voltage calculations, and spot welding. I also deconstructed and repaired in-wheel electric motors on a professional longboard.

Heavy Duty Robotic Arm

I researched, designed, and begun construction of a heavy duty robotic arm capable of picking up objects weighing 10 pounds or less across a lenght of 1.5 feet.

  • Reviewed the basics and limitations of robotic arms, conventional and stepper motors, servos, motor drivers, H-bridges, and learned to permantently wire up single-board computers (i.e. Raspberry Pi).
  • Begun the construciton of a robotic arm driven by linear acutators, H-bridges, and headless Raspberry Pi taking into consideration linear acutators duty cycles, weight limits, and the Raspberry Pis GPIO constraints.

Electirc Longboard

With the recent popularization of longboards, I decided to build my own electric longboard and upgrade the batteries for another.

  • Retrofitted an electric motor, belt drive assembly, and belt tensioner to a normal longboard. Acheieved a speed of 7 miles per hour with interchangeable batteries. Decided to not use in-wheel motors due to project budget.
  • Built custom battery packs for an elerctic longboard by thorughly researching batteries, calculating voltage needs, spot welding lithium batteries in a series, attaching a battery management system, ensuring comptability with regenerative braking, and updating the input cable on the original board.
  • Deconstructed and fixed in-wheel electirc motors on a professional electric longboard.
