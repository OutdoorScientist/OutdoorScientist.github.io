Near-Surface Geophysics

I led an electromagnetic profiling (EM) survey as part of a geophysical project for the Virginia Department of Transportation, using magnetics, EM, and ground-penetrating radar (GPR) methodologies. I coordinated data acquisition, processed and mapped results for cross-method analysis, and gained experience managing field operations.

Additionally, I participated in over a dozen near-surface geophysical surveys, utilizing methods like Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT), Seismic, and Induced Polarization (IP). I processed, analyzed, and presented multi-axis data and gained skills in academic writing and presenting geophysical findings.

Virginia Department of Transportation Survey

I traveled to Virginia with Dr. Mark Everett to conduct a geophysical survey for the Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT). The survey methodologies conducted included magnetics, electromagnetic profiling (EM), and ground penetrating radar (GPR). I led the EM data acquisition due to my experience using the Electromagnetic Profiler.

  • Learned how to prepare for and conduct multiple geophysical surveys back to back in a short duration.
  • Processed and mapped the data in a format that all the methodologies could be evaluated side by side.
  • Learned to clearly outline survey requirements and manage the users while leading the EM Profiling.
